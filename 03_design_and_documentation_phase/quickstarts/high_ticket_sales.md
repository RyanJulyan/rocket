# High Ticket Sales: The Big Money Script
> Adapted from [**The Pocket Guide to High Ticket Selling:** _A 12-Phase Sales Script You Can Use to Sell Millions Over the Phone_ By Dan Henry](https://www.amazon.com/Pocket-Guide-High-Ticket-Selling-ebook/dp/B0BGMQF5SJ)

## Overview

The **Big Money Script** is a 12-phase framework for selling high-ticket products and services over the phone. This is not a rigid script to follow blindly—it's a framework to gather information and make customized pitches. Skip sections you don't need. **If a customer is ready to buy, let them buy.** Do not force them through the entire script.

### Golden Rules
- Say as little as possible—let prospects do 95% of the talking
- If a prospect is ready to buy at any point, close the sale
- Gather information to make a customized pitch, not a generic one
- In chaos there is no sale; only in clarity is there a sale

## Phase 1: Rapport
### Purpose
Build trust and connection during the first few minutes of the call through genuine conversation.

### Key Tactics
- **Find Common Ground:** Ask where they're from, how their week is going, what they're interested in
- **Research First:** Look them up on social media to find shared interests you can reference
- **Match Their Energy:** If they're reserved, be reserved. If they're high energy, match that. People buy from people they relate to
- **Connect on Values:** Establish genuine connection before moving to business

### Example
> "Yeah, I get that. No way I'm putting my kid through that either. I'd rather homeschool."
> 
> Then they say, "Totally. It's crazy what they are doing now."
> 
> **Bam.** Common ground achieved.

## Phase 2: Expectations
### Purpose
Set the format and flow of the call so you can maintain control and keep the conversation on track.

### Key Tactics
- **Frame the Call:** "So, this is how these calls usually go..."
- **Get Agreement:** Get them to agree to the format before proceeding
- **Create Clarity:** Without framing, conversations turn into chaos
- **Easy Redirect:** Once they've agreed to a format, you can easily redirect them back to it

### Example Script
**You:** "Are you ready to get started, [First Name]?"
**Them:** "Sure."
**You:** "Great. So here's how these calls usually go... [outline format]. Does that sound good?"

---

## Phase 3: Decision Makers
### Purpose
Identify all decision makers and get them on the call if possible.

### Key Tactics
- **Get Everyone On:** If only one decision maker is present, they'll relay information poorly and won't handle objections
- **Watch the Silent Killer:** In partnerships/couples, identify the quieter one—they often make the final "no"
- **Include the Quiet One:** Make sure they feel heard, ask them questions, get their objections so you can address them
- **Avoid Restart:** Don't give all attention to one person; you don't want to start over with the other at close time

### Questions to Ask
- "Besides you, who else would be involved in making this decision?"
- "Is your partner/colleague comfortable with the investment?"

---

## Phase 4: The Reason
### Purpose
Get the prospect to verbalize why they're on the call and the pain surrounding that reason.

### Key Tactics
- **They Must Say It:** Even if it seems obvious, they need to state the reason out loud
- **Find the Pain:** People buy when the cost of the pain exceeds the cost of solving it
- **Ask Why Now:** "Why is it so important to solve this right now?"
- **Get Specific:** Go deeper if they give generic answers

### Example
**You:** "Why did you book this call today? What specifically do you need help with?"
**Them:** "I saw your founder did $30M in sales and I need help selling more without doing all the calls myself."
**You:** "Why now specifically?"
**Them:** "My baby was just born and I can't take sales calls all day, but I don't know how to hire or manage a sales team."

**Now you've found the real pain: Family time, not just more sales.**

---

## Phase 5: Dig
### Purpose
Deepen emotional connection to the pain so they feel the weight of not solving it.

### Key Tactics
- **Get Specific Details:** If they give a general answer, ask: "Can you expand on that? Tell me more."
- **Explore Root Causes:** Why do they think this problem exists? How long has it been a problem?
- **Show Impact:** How is this affecting other areas of their life?
- **Use "What/How" Not "Why":** Avoid asking "Why?" as it triggers defensive responses (childhood programming)
  - Better: "What makes you say that?" or "How did you come to that conclusion?"

### Probe Questions
- What have you tried to fix it?
- How long has this been a problem?
- Is this affecting your life in other ways, and how?

---

## Phase 6: Tried
### Purpose
Understand what solutions they've already attempted so you can position your offer as different.

### Key Tactics
- **Ask at Emotional Peak:** By now they're emotionally engaged, so they'll share more information
- **Identify Gaps:** Know what they've tried so you can explain why your solution is different
- **Preempt Objections:** If they've tried X, acknowledge it and explain why your approach is superior
- **Avoid Commodity Positioning:** Don't say "we do what they do" - explain what makes you different

### Example
Instead of: "We show you how to cold call event organizers"
Say: "We give you our proven cold script—a script 99% of speakers don't use because they don't know it exists. I know you've tried cold calls before, but with our script, you're shocked at how quickly you book gigs."

---

## Phase 7: Current and Desired Situation
### Purpose
Establish where they are now and where they want to be using quantifiable metrics.

### Key Tactics
- **Use Metrics:** Ask specific, measurable questions
  - Making money? "How much are you currently making?"
  - Losing weight? "What's your current weight?"
  - Speaking engagements? "How many gigs are you booking?"
- **Set Realistic Goals:** Help them establish achievable targets
- **Paint a Picture:** Describe what success looks like in their terms
- **Handle Unrealistic Expectations:** If they want too much too fast, reset without dismissing them

### Reset Script for Unrealistic Expectations
> "I understand you want [unrealistic outcome]. That's fantastic. But just to set realistic expectations: if we achieved [realistic outcome], would you be more than happy?"

---

## Phase 8: Why
### Purpose
Understand the deeper "why" behind their desired outcome—what does the result really mean to them?

### Key Tactics
- **Dig Deeper:** "You want more money—what will you do with it? Why?"
- **Find the Real Motivation:** Often it's not money, it's family, legacy, or avoiding disappointment
- **Use "What Would That Look Like?"** Non-aggressive way to get specifics without seeming nosy
- **Associate with Results:** "So you'd be happy if you made an extra million dollars—what would that look like?"

### Common Deeper Motivations
- Love (family, relationships)
- Status (recognition, legacy)
- Belonging (community, acceptance)
- Avoiding loss (not letting down family, not being a disappointment)

### Example
**Surface:** "I want to make more money"
**Dig:** "What would that look like? What would change?"
**Real Pain:** "My wife has been asking for a bigger house for years. When I can finally buy it for her, that moment will mean everything."

---

## Phase 9: Admission
### Purpose
Get them to admit they need help and can't solve this alone.

### Key Tactics
- **Critical Phase:** If they don't admit they need help, you cannot sell them
- **Must Say It Aloud:** They need to hear themselves say it
- **Ask Directly:** "What's stopping you from achieving this all on your own without any help?"
- **Expected Answers:** You're looking for one of three responses:
  1. "I don't know how to do it"
  2. "I want to follow a proven process from someone who's already done it"
  3. "I want to get there faster"

---

## Phase 10: Commitment
### Purpose
Secure their commitment to solve the problem now, not later.

### Key Tactics
- **Test Commitment:** "How committed are you to making this happen? Will you do the work? Will you take action? Are you coachable?"
- **Handle Delays:** If they say "maybe next month," dig in:
  - "What makes you say a month?"
  - "You booked a call for today—why wait?"
  - "Any reason you don't want to fix this right now?"
- **Use the Procrastination Close:** If they still hesitate, show them past patterns of procrastination

### Procrastination Close
> "Would you say it's accurate that you've been trying to solve this for [X time]? And in that time, it hasn't gotten better, right? What makes you think waiting another month will be different?"

### Quality Over Speed
- Don't pressure someone who isn't ready
- Better to lose a weak sale than gain a difficult client
- You want people who will take action and get results (good for them and your reputation)

---

## Phase 11: Customized Close
### Purpose
Deliver a tailored pitch that feels like the offer was made specifically for them.

### Key Tactics
- **Use Their Language:** Describe the customer identity as they described themselves, the problem as they described it
- **Reference Gathered Information:** Weave in details from earlier phases (their pain, goals, why it matters)
- **Focus on Relevance:** Only talk about the parts of your solution that solve their specific problem
- **Tell Them How It Works:** Explain the process, timeline, and what to expect
- **Answer Questions First:** After your pitch, ask "Do you have any questions?" and answer only what they ask

### Structure
1. **Identity:** "Someone like you who [specific description they gave]"
2. **Problem:** "Facing [specific problem they mentioned]"
3. **Solution:** "What we do is [how you solve it for them specifically]"
4. **Process:** "Here's how it works: [timeline and steps]"
5. **Stop Talking:** Don't add extra information they didn't ask for

---

## Phase 12: Price
### Purpose
Reveal the investment in a way that feels justified by all the value discussed.

### Key Tactics
- **Never Volunteer Price:** Wait until they ask
- **Keep Answering Questions:** They'll ask about price eventually—don't rush it
- **When They Ask:** Pause after stating the price and say nothing
- **The Most Important Rule:** **DO NOT SPEAK** after quoting the price
  - Let them talk themselves into it or ask follow-ups
  - A prospect may go from "I can't afford this" to "I can't afford NOT to do this" on their own (often in 100+ seconds)
  - Common next statement: "Do you take MasterCard?"

### After Price is Stated
- They either buy, deposit, or need to overcome an objection
- Be prepared to schedule follow-up or discuss payment plans
- If they get a payment plan, don't make it hard

## The Objection Phase

While a perfectly executed script should minimize objections, they will happen. Here are common objections and how to handle them:

### Objection 1: "I've Tried Products Like This Before"
**Problem:** They're not convinced you're different.

**Poor Response:** Talking about how great your product is, how many features it has, how many testimonials—THIS IS WRONG.

**Better Response:** Get specific about what they tried and why it didn't work. Then show how you're different.

**Example:**
> "That's great that you've already tried solutions. I'm curious—what specifically didn't work about it? ... Okay, I see. Here's what's different about our approach: [specific differentiation based on what failed before]"

### Objection 2: "I'm On Vacation Next Month, I'll Wait"
**Problem:** They don't want to waste money on a service they can't use.

**Solution (Concession Close):** Add value that costs you little but solves their concern.

**Example:**
> "I understand you don't want to lose a month's worth of service. Here's what I'll do: enroll today and I'll add an extra month onto your contract. Plus, if you want to access the members area while on vacation, you can consider that bonus time. Does that work?"

### Objection 3: "I Want to Talk to Your Clients First"
**Problem:** Trust issue—either you have no testimonials or they don't trust the ones you show.

**Solution:** Use the "Liar Close" to move them past doubt.

> "I totally understand. Look, I could give you client references, but honestly, if I were a liar, wouldn't I just give you my brother-in-law to pose as a client? The real question is: deep down, you already know what you need to do. Do you want help from someone who's already done it, or do you want to keep figuring it out alone?"

## BONUS: The Possession Close

### When to Use
When the prospect is still on the fence after you've done everything else. They're interested but hesitant to commit.

### Framework
Offer them a trial period where they get full access to the product before committing to payment.

**Example Script:**
> "I understand you're nervous about making this investment. So here's what I'll do for you. I'll give you a 24-hour pass to fully experience the product before you buy. You can log in, watch the modules, network with the other members, and even make a post that asks if everyone thought their investment was worth it. We'll schedule a follow-up call 24 hours from now. On that call you can decide if you'd like to keep the program or not. If so, we'll process your payment and let you keep access. If not, we'll revoke access and part as friends. Does that work for you?"

### The Psychology Behind It
The name comes from a bar strategy: shot girls would physically hand a shot to a customer before asking if they wanted it. Once the customer had physical possession of the shot, it was much harder for them to say no. They almost always said yes.

This principle translates to digital products—once prospects have possession and can experience the value firsthand, they're much more likely to say yes.

### Key Points
- Only give access to premium content (make it "juicy")
- Most prospects won't download all your content in 24 hours
- If you're worried about content theft, just give access to a portion of the program
- Set a specific follow-up call time (24 hours later)
- This tactic has been used to generate millions in additional enrollments

### Real Example
Dan Henry used this close in his online course business to sell an extra **$1 million worth of enrollments per year**.

## BONUS: The 1 to 10 Close

### When to Use
When you have authority in your space and a logical path showing they'll succeed faster with you than alone.

### Framework
1. **Establish Authority:** "I've helped hundreds do this..."
2. **Ask the Logic Questions:** "If you had [your help], would you get results faster? Would it be easier?"
3. **Build the Case:** Get them saying "yes" to increasingly specific questions
4. **State the Obvious:** "So it makes sense to work together, right? On a scale of 1-10, how much does that make sense?"

### Key Point
Most people already know the right answer deep down—they just need to say it out loud to make a decision.

## An Important Note about Marketing

**Marketing makes sales both possible and easier.**

- The better your marketing, lead generation, content, and copywriting, the easier closing becomes
- If you generate leads without credibility, clarity, or authority, your sales calls will be hard
- Good marketing establishes that you know what you're talking about before the call even starts
- Prospects should know, like, and trust you before picking up the phone

### The Flywheel
- Better marketing → More qualified leads → Easier closes → More results → Better testimonials → Better marketing
- Weak marketing → Weak leads → Harder closes → Fewer results → Weak testimonials → Harder marketing
